    Introduction
    In today's fast-paced technology based environment, smart homes are becoming increasingly common. Smart home devices allow greater control over other housing devices and enable comfortable living.
    The same comfort and control should also be available to the visually impaired. Google Home is a smart speaker that allows the blind population to do so through spoken commands.
    Using this smart speaker allows for many advantages in terms of
  • managing daily tasks easily
  • using simple features (such as stopwatches, etc)
  • entertainment (plays music, podcasts, audiobooks, etc)
    		•

    Smart Home
    Google Home allows for extensive control over a range of smart home devices.

    It can monitor temperature, lighting, audio for external speakers, etc.

    In terms of smart home control, Google Home ranks higher than other products due to the number of devices it can monitor.

    It also supports control of smart switches and plugs such as electronic juicers and coffee makers.

    Using Google Home can allow even the visually imapaired to have complete control over their Smart Home devices.

    Since Google Home is operated by voice commands, it is relatively easy to control a Smart Home.

    Entertainment with Google Home
    Google Home not only allows for control over a Smart Home but contains many entertainment functions, specificlly accessible to the visually impaired.
    Various entertainment features are available with Google Home. It can either provide the entertainment itself, control the entertainment device or generally access the platform.
    Using Google Home allows for
  • listening to audiobooks sirectly from the speaker
  • listening to podcasts and music from platforms such as Spotify
  • asking for riddles and/or jokes from Google Home
  • asking for interesting factual knowledge and information
  • listening to news station radio broadcasts on platforms such as BBC

    Cost of Google Home
    Compared to other Smart Speakers, Google Home is a more affordble device. Its main compeitors - Amazon Echo and Apple Homepod are all priced above $200.
    The Google Home smart speaker costs around $189, making it under the ideal price for such a device.
    The average income for the visually impaired is SG$55,000. Therefore, approximately $1650 will be spent on all such services and apparel.
    The cost for the smart home products should ideally be below $200 as other apparel and their costs should be considered.

    Availability
    Google Home has a major online prevalence as it is sold on many online shopping platforms, resellers and retail stores worldwide.
    It is easily available to purchase in most countries and urbanised areas.
    As for language support, Google Home is available in five of the most spoken languages other than English which include French, German, Japanese, Spanish and Italian.
    Translation features are also supported in this using Google Translate.
